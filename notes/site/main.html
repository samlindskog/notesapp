1 Foundations of Sequence and Series

1.1 Sequence and Series

A Sequence is a list of numbers a1,a2,a3,…,an,…. An Infinite Series is the sum of an infinite sequence of numbers. The sum of the first n terms, called the nth partial sum, is Sn. The sequence of partial sums of this series is:

Sn=a1+a2+…+an=∑k=1nak

1.2 Geometric Series

Geometric Series are series of the form a+a⁢r+a⁢r2+…+a⁢rn−1+…, a and r are real numbers, r is the common ratio, and a≠0. The nth partial sum of a geometric series can be written as:

Sn=a⁢(1−rn)1−r,(r≠1)

Infinite geometric series in summation form:

∑n=1∞a⁢rn−1

2 Convergence and Divergence

2.1 Convergent and Divergent Sequence and Series

A sequence {an} converges to a number L if:

(∀ε∈ℝ,ε>0)⁢(∃N∈ℤ)⁢(n>N⟹|an−L|<ε)

If no such number L exists, {an} diverges.


If a sequence of partial sums Sn of an infinite series converges to a limit L, we say that the series converges and that it’s sum is L, i.e.

∑k=1∞ak=L

If a sequence of partial sums does not converge, it diverges.

2.2 Convergence and Divergence Tests

Divergence Test

If l⁢i⁢mn→∞⁢{an}≠0, then the series ∑ak diverges. If limn→∞{an}=0, no conclusion can be made.

Integral Test

Let f be a positive, decreasing function on the interval [c,∞), and (k∈ℤ)⁢(ak=f⁢(k)⁢∣k>⁢c):

if⁢∫c∞f⁢(t)⁢𝑑t⁢ converges, then⁢∑ak⁢ converges
if⁢∫c∞f⁢(t)⁢𝑑t⁢ diverges, then⁢∑ak⁢ diverges

Direct Comparison Test

If (∀k∈ℤ)⁢(0≤ak≤bk), then:

If⁢∑bk⁢ converges, then⁢∑ak⁢ converges.
If⁢∑ak⁢ diverges, then⁢∑bk⁢ diverges.

Limit Comparison Test

Let an and bn be sequences of positive terms. If limk→∞akbk=L, for a positive finite number L, then both series either both converge or both diverge.

Ratio Test

Let ak≠0 for each k, and limk→∞|ak+1|ak=r:

If ⁢r<1⁢, then the series⁢∑ak⁢ converges absolutely.
If ⁢r>1⁢, then the series⁢∑ak⁢ diverges.
If ⁢r=1⁢, then the series⁢∑ak⁢ the test is inconclusive

Root Test

Let ak≥0 for each k, and limk→∞akk=r:

If ⁢r<1⁢, then⁢∑ak⁢ converges.
If ⁢r>1⁢, then⁢∑ak⁢ diverges.
If ⁢r=1⁢, then⁢∑ak⁢ the test is inconclusive.

Alternating Series Test

If an is a positive, decreasing sequence so that limn→∞an=0, then ∑(−1)k+1⁢ak  converges.

Alternating Series Estimation Theorem

Let Sn be the nth partial sum of the alternating series ∑k=1∞(−1)k+1⁢ak, where:

(∀n∈ℤ,n>0)⁢(an>0)⁢(limn→∞an=0)⁢(limn→∞Sn=S)

Then if follows that:

|S−Sn|<an+1

2.3 Absolute and Conditional Convergence

If for a series ∑ak, ∑|ak| converges, then the series is absolutely convergent. If ∑|ak| diverges, but ∑ak converges, then the series is conditionally convergent.

3 Taylor and Maclaurin Series

3.1 Taylor Polynomials

Taylor Polynomials are useful because they allow us to calculate the value of hard to compute functions using only addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division operators. The nth order Taylor polynomial of f centered at x=a is given by:

Pn⁢(x) =f⁢(a)+f′⁢(a)⁢(x−a)+f′′⁢(a)2!⁢(x−a)2+…+f(n)⁢(a)n!⁢(x−a)n
=∑k=0nf(k)⁢(a)n!⁢(x−a)k

3.2 Taylor and Maclaurin Series

The Taylor Series for f centered at x=a is:

Tf⁢(x)=∑k=0∞f(k)⁢(a)k!⁢(x−a)k

When a=0, the taylor series is also called the Maclaurin Series for f

3.3 Interval and Radius of Convergence

The interval of convergence for a Taylor Series refers to the interval of x for which Tf⁢(x) converges to f⁢(x). The interval of x for which a Taylor Series converges is always centered at x=a, and half the length of this interval is the radius of convergence.

3.4 Lagrange Error Bound for Nth Degree Taylor Polynomial

Let f be a continuous function with n+1 continuous derivatives. Suppose that M is a positive real number such that |f(n−1)⁢(x)|≤M on the interval [a,c]. If Pn⁢(x) is the nth order Taylor polynomial for f⁢(x) centered at x=a, then:

|Pn⁢(c)−f⁢(c)|≤M⁢|c−a|n+1(n+1)!
